Studio Myo

Studio Myo is a dynamic digital studio focused on innovation, quality, efficiency, and adaptability. We strive to create fresh, engaging experiences that push the boundaries of design and technology, always ready to evolve with the needs of our clients.

The Challange

The challenge was to create and brand ourselves as a full digital studio, ready to engage with real-world clients. We were tasked with developing a unique studio name, defining the type of studio we wanted to be, and building a cohesive brand identity that would represent our values and skills in the digital space.

To Start

We initially set up a Trello board to split tasks among group members, but after receiving feedback from our coaches, we switched to Jira, recognizing it as a more professional tool for task management. We then created a Team Contract to discuss our strengths and weaknesses, as well as establish rules for attendance and communication, ensuring that we worked in a professional and organized manner.


I then began defining the brand's core values to create a strong foundation for the design elements, ensuring they aligned with the brand’s identity and message.

Design

After defining the core values, I began designing the logo. My goal was to create a modern, minimalist design that was both impactful and more creative than a simple text-based logo. I drew inspiration from Pinterest, exploring the use of positive and negative space. To organize my ideas, I compiled my inspirations and color palette into a stylescape to present to my group.


After receiving feedback and reviewing my team members' designs, I began exploring new logo ideas, focusing on the core value of 'adaptability' and conveying a sense of 'flexibility'. Drawing inspiration from the logos in my stylescape, I experimented with negative space to create more dynamic and visually engaging designs. To refine my concepts and enhance my proficiency with professional tools, I developed these logos using Adobe Illustrator and they can be seen below in the first blueprint image.


After some discussion, our team decided to slightly shift the direction of our studio’s branding. We realized that our current branding lacked individuality, so we chose to incorporate a chameleon as our mascot—a symbol of adaptability and versatility. With this new focus, I revisited the logo design process, experimenting with ways to integrate the chameleon into our visual identity which is seen below in the second blueprint image.

Colours

Once we had each completed our stylescapes and logo concepts, I took the initiative to develop a cohesive color palette for our brand. We initially gravitated toward blue tones, as we felt they best represented our studio’s identity. However, each of our palettes featured unique shades of blue paired with different accent colors.


While refining the final palette, I carefully combined elements from each of our designs to create a balanced and distinctive look. Although our mascot was a chameleon, we intentionally avoided using green, as it felt too predictable and lacked the originality we wanted for our brand.

Brand Guide

After extensive iteration and discussion, we decided on a logo designed by one of my teammates, as it best captured our brand’s identity and character. To ensure consistency across all branding elements, I collaborated with a teammate to develop a comprehensive brand guide. This guide outlined our logo usage, typography, color palette, design elements, and overall visual direction, serving as a foundation for maintaining a cohesive and professional brand identity.


I also created some poster options for the brand and made the motivational document to send to the client.
